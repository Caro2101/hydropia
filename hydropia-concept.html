Hydroponics : Concept

How does it works?

A new way to produce vegetables

Hydroponics is an above-ground production technique, which means that the roots of the cultivated plants do not plunge into their natural environment (the soil), but into a nutritious liquid. The origin of the word "hydroponics" comes from the Greek "hydro", water and "ponos", work. This technique appears as "the work of the roots in the water".

Concretely, the mineral nutrition of the plant is ensured by a nutritive solution administered to the roots, providing the essential elements for the growth of any plant: water, dissolved oxygen and mineral elements (contained in the water).

The technique

Aeroponics : A suspended and constantly irrigated agriculture

Aeroponics is another water based system, which, like NFT, requires no medium. Plants are suspended on a tray, with their roots freely dangling below. The entire tray is placed into a box that has a small amount of water and nutrient solution in the bottom. A pump system is used to draw the water up, where it's sprayed in a fine mist onto the entire plant and root in a continuous manner.

This system is the most difficult to set up and manage, but it has great potential for large commercial uses.

Benefits of Hydroponics

Three main advantages

Use of water

The water is implement in a circular process, so it is recycled. There, Hydroponics allow to be up to 90% more efficient use of water.

Grows faster

Many crops can be produced twice as fast in a well managed hydroponic system. In addition, production increases 3 to 10 times in the same amount of space.

Zero soil

Since it's a suspended culture there is no need for soil. Furthermore, no chemical weed or pest control products are needed when operating a hydroponic system.
